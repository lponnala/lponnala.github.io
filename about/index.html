About

I am a quantitative analyst in the NYC finance industry.

Previously, I was a postdoctoral researcher at Cornell University where I worked on computational problems in high-throughput genomics and quantitative proteomics. During my time in Ithaca, I was a resident of Fall Creek, a member of the local Toastmasters club, a volunteer for CCE-Tompkins, and a fairly regular attendee at the Canaan Institute old-time/folk jam sessions.

I completed my PhD at NCSU in ECE (major) and Statistics (minor). I was a member of the Bioinformatics Lab where I developed a signal processing model for analyzing ribosomal frameshifts.
